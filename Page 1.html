He walked till he reached the Waterfall, and sat on a stone near it. Reading the book with little curiosity, having many pieces of history he knows, yet some he doesn't comprehend. He read chapters, giving meanings to the forgotten past, and discovering early experiments directing to the present. After reading countless pages, Gaster saw instructions written in the language of the hands, Wingdings. Believing Gerson needed help from a skeleton to write it, for they're the ones who understand the symbols.

He read it in a whisper. "Go to Winter'd Inn and there you may find a device, which can see the visions no normal monsters notices with their eyes. You'll see a sector without a sign, go there. Once you found it, it's yours to hold and keep."

Gaster closed the book. He was tired, still less curious. The task would be done another day as he has work to do in the Lab. Though instead of walking this time, he teleported to the laboratory where the subject is contained in a compartment. Gaster created this experiment of his a few months ago for monster research. Tomorrow he'll release it to test its stability and statistics a second time, for a week to understand the possibility of making a stronger form of bioweapon.

"We meet again, insolent one," Gaster said.
